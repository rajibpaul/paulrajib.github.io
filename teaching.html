Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

In both teaching and mentoring research projects, my philosophy is that all life experiences teach us something. Keeping this in mind, I always endeavor to improve the learning experience of a student during class time, during office hours, and during out-of-class mentoring. We all start teaching with the experience of being taught. My experience of good tuition combines many small moments during my undergraduate, graduate, and Ph.D. courses. The enthusiasm from my honorable mentors renewed my own. This cherished experience has shaped the backbone of my approach to teaching being generous to students and explaining the subject passionately. This philosophy maneuvers my teaching goals:

  • Well-developed course material to ensure that the students learn from it.
  • Integration of research, education, and service into teaching.
  • Utilization of different teaching methods, technology, and real-world applications for success.
  • Prepare well to handle difficult and new ideas at an appropriate pace during the lecture.
  • Actively make efforts to improve student confidence.
  • Strictly on schedule to be approachable and available to all the students from different courses

I accomplish this goal through departmental and campus resources, SMART (specific, measurable, attainable, realistic, timely) learning objectives, and teaching tools.
